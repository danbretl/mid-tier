Kwiqet creates a market that matches event businesses who have perishable inventory with targeted consumers looking for fairly priced tickets. With our unique blend of engineering talent, design prowess and marketing savvy, we are revolutionizing the ticketing industry by building a product that is more exciting and fair for the consumer, and more effective and lucrative for the business.

Team

The Kwiqet team is guided by one simple word: teamwork. Our whole philosophy revolves around collaboration, the idea of great thinkers from a variety of different fields coming together to create technology that makes people's lives easier and more efficient. With Kwiqet, we are able to effectively collaborate each and every day.

Matt Hooper | President and CEO

is a graduate of New York University. He is the creator of the web series "GoCause" which was featured prominently in the 2010 American Express "Members Project" campaign, where the series generated over $500,000 for participating non-profit organizations Rails-to-Trails Conservancy and Jumpstart. "GoCause" will also serve as the national "Big Brothers Big Sisters: Do Something" campaign. A contributing blogger at The Huffington Post, Matt has worked in the political arena, both for U.S. Senator Chuck Schumer (D-NY) and for President Barack Obama's 2008 campaign.

Pavel Katsev | VP Engineering

studied operations research at UIUC with a focus on transportation optimization algorithms. Since 2006, he has worked with the architecture teams at Garmin in San Francisco and UPS in the Midwest, dealing with large datasets, geospatial systems and Internet technologies. After leaving Garmin in 2009, he joined Nixle, a San Francisco-based company specializing in mobile messaging among geographic and interest-based user groups, where he built applications from the ground up and scaled them for production.

Ryan Wegner | Relations Manager

is a graduate of Columbia University. He is currently earning his Ph.D in Clinical Psychology at Columbia. He co-founded and was CEO of a nonprofit corporation called the Art Kart. He is also a professional musician who has had extensive experience in the music industry and continues to be heavily involved in the New York music scene.

Dan Bretl | UI/UX Developer

is a graduate of Sarah Lawrence College, where he studied computer science, mathematics, and visual art. He has self-published several iPhone and iPad apps, taking projects from initial front-end sketches all the way to backend data models and web APIs. His personal work is focused on using technology to enable others to have creative cross-disciplinary experiences. Dan has also run a freelance photography business for six years while living in New York, Alaska, France, Australia and Bangladesh, with the rest of the world squeezed somewhere in between.
